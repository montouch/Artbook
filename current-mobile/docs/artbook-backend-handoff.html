1. Product Summary

Artbook is a creator-first social, commerce, audio, live, booking and collaboration app. It blends artist profiles, streamer subscription vaults, business services, marketplace listings, events, GPS/manual local discovery, Tour Mode, personal messages, wallet flows and collaborative rooms.

Creator identity

Artists, streamers, businesses, creators and institutions have role-aware profile pages, statuses, privacy settings and studio analytics.

Commerce

Products, services, tickets, digital assets, classes and subscriptions have search, filters, reviews, upload metadata, delivery checks, business-model walk-ins, POS register operations and QR sale paths.

Rooms

Messages, audio rooms, video/audio calls, listen-together sessions and collaboration rooms share a communication model.

Trust

Identity, content rights, delivery route checks, Provenance Seals, forwarding consent and booking protocols are visible in the prototype.

2. Prototype Boundary

7. QR Pickup Queue

1
Ask
Customer asks ahead. This creates a queue request only, with no release code.
2
Hold
Seller accepts or declines. Accepted holds reserve stock/capacity until a clear expiry window.
3
Arrive
Customer reaches the shop/counter. Seller confirms presence before release.
4
Release
Seller creates a short-lived counter code, verifies it, then moves the item into checkout.

8. Booking Protocol

1
Book
Booker chooses provider, service and slot. Server creates confirmed booking and deposit/payment state.
2
Reschedule
Server records previous slot, new slot, note, actor and whether provider approval is needed.
3
Cancel
Server applies cancellation policy, refund percentage, wallet/payment adjustment and notifies both sides.
4
Audit
All booking changes are immutable audit events.

9. Trust And Safety Rules

  • Money actions must require identity and server-side ledger checks.
  • Artist uploads need rights declarations: original work, controlled label catalog or written permission.
  • Streamer subscription vaults need identity, age/content policy and consent checks for people shown.
  • Business selling requires seller verification, payout setup, refund terms and delivery policy.
  • QR sale tokens must be server-owned, expiring and revocable. Customer prompt codes should be created only when the customer is at the shop, then used to release the queued item once.
  • Provenance Seals should count only when tied to orders, bookings, collabs, venue proof, delivery proof, identity review or trusted local relationships.
  • Low seller score or open reports should trigger listing review, checkout hold, payout delay or manual investigation.
  • Forwarding content depends on creator permission, collaboration state and content license.
  • Location ranking must respect user privacy: exact GPS, locality, city or hidden.
  • Direct business calls are blocked unless caller is a business, artist or streamer; otherwise book consultation.

10. Build Roadmap

1
API and database
Move profiles, feed, listings, bookings, messages and collabs to PostgreSQL-backed APIs.
2
Media and realtime
Add object storage, signed upload URLs, realtime messaging and room presence.
3
Trust providers
Wire KYC, Provenance Seals, seller scoring, moderation, reporting, admin queues and audit logs.
4
Commerce
Implement orders, reviews, QR sale prompts with counter-only release codes, tickets, subscriptions, Google Play Billing and payout settlement.
5
Location, delivery and bookings
Add geocoding/privacy, delivery quotes/tracking and full booking state machine with refunds.
6
Release engineering
Convert to release-grade Android project, add signing/versioning, privacy policy, terms and Play Console assets.

Play Store note:

The prototype APK is useful for showing the product on a phone. It should not be submitted to Play Store as-is. Before launch, the app needs production backend services, release signing, privacy/data-safety work, Google Play Billing where required, moderation tools and compliance documentation.
