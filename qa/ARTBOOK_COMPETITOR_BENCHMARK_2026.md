# Artbook 2026 Product Benchmark

Purpose: keep the Artbook prototype current against adjacent apps while preserving its own shape.

## Similar Product Expectations

### Social + Creator Discovery

- TikTok Shop expectation: products can be discovered inside short video/live content, sellers and creators have distinct commerce roles, orders/fulfillment/refunds are traceable, and affiliate or creator claims need report/compliance paths.
- Instagram/YouTube Studio expectation: creator dashboards should make content performance, notifications, comments/messages, publishing health, copyright/monetization checks, and next actions easy to scan.
- Spotify for Artists expectation: artists need audience segments, release/profile tools, campaign prompts, and clear analytics that help convert casual listeners into active fans.

Artbook check: Circle, live rooms, audio, podcasts, subscriptions, Studio analytics, rights notes, creator roles, and trust/Provenance must stay tied together without overwhelming first-time users.

### Marketplace + Seller Operations

- Shopify POS expectation: online/offline sales, inventory, pickup/local delivery, customer profiles, receipts, staff permissions, payments, and reporting should feel unified.
- Square POS/Appointments expectation: mobile POS, invoices, inventory, team permissions, activity logs, sales reports, appointment reminders, contracts, offline payments, and customer data are owner-facing essentials.
- Etsy Seller expectation: listings, messages, orders, stats, revenue, and customer/order details must be reachable from the seller dashboard without confusion.

Artbook check: Sales Desk, QR/guest orders, receipts/invoices, accounting readout, inventory, basket, Today purchase cards, customer letters, notifications, follow-ups, emails, and staff/customer state should feel like one owner workflow.

### Booking + Service Businesses

- Acuity/Square expectation: booking flows need client profiles, intake forms, appointment history, packages/subscriptions/gift certificates, reminders, payment collection, staff/location calendars, recurring bookings, reschedule/cancel rules, and follow-up emails.

Artbook check: Calendar, Booking Desk, customer intake, policies, staff/resources, client-side confirmations, provider prep, reminders, and after-care should be explicit and not buried.

### Messaging + Customer Care

- WhatsApp Business expectation: customer chat should support business profile/catalog, quick replies, welcome/away messages, labels, item links, and external customer handoff without forcing every buyer into a complex account flow.
- Patreon expectation: membership/chat/community areas should connect creators with paying/free supporters and expose membership/shop insights.

Artbook check: Inbox/Letters, Rooms, follow-ups, email drafts, guest records, open-door links, subscriptions, parent/child and assisted purchase flows need clear permissions and readable states.

### Delivery + Trust

- 2026 commerce expectation: pickup/delivery availability should be checked before payment capture, proof of delivery should be attached to the order, and disputes/scams/affiliate claims need reportable trails.

Artbook check: Route Canvas, courier portal, QR pickup queue, counter release codes, proof requirements, payout holds, seller trust, reports, and Provenance Seals should always point to a specific order/booking/collab/event.

## Audit Priorities

1. New user path: can a creator/customer understand Home, Circle, Create, Inbox, Menu in under a minute?
2. Seller path: can a business sell, invoice, receipt, follow up, and see accounting without losing the customer record?
3. Buyer path: can a buyer add to basket, checkout, track pickup/delivery/digital entitlement, and message the seller?
4. Booking path: can a customer book/reschedule/cancel and can the provider see prep, payment, policy, and reminders?
5. Subscription/media path: can a streamer/artist publish or subscribe without confusing free/paid/rights boundaries?
6. Courier path: can a rider see shift, offers, route, proof, incidents, and payout state?
7. Account isolation: purchases/subscriptions/messages/orders should not leak into the wrong demo account.
8. UI naturalness: first screen should prioritize the next action, reduce text weight, avoid dead buttons, and keep touch targets reliable.
9. Production honesty: fake-local money, identity, media, delivery, calls, and rights checks must be visibly demo-only/backend-owned.

## Current Source Spot-Check - Fundi Jobs, Terms And Provenance

- Upwork fixed-price/milestone guidance frames work around agreed deliverables, due dates, payment amounts, escrow funding and review before release: `https://support.upwork.com/hc/en-us/articles/44564821903763-Understanding-milestones-on-fixed-price-contracts`.
- Freelancer-style project marketplaces separate bidding/quotes from milestone-funded delivery and disputeable payment release: `https://www.freelancer.com/support/Payments/what-is-the-milestone-payment-system`.
- Taskrabbit-style local-service terms keep payment and task communication on-platform, with price/task details agreed between client and tasker: `https://support.taskrabbit.com/hc/en-us/articles/204411610-How-Do-I-Pay-My-Tasker`.

Artbook implication for this pass: Fundi Jobs should not let "accept bid" mean "start work". A bid is a quote. The safer model is quote -> two-sided agreement -> customer-funded escrow -> held work -> proof -> customer completion -> delayed Seal decision. Price, scope, timing and proof changes become change orders requiring the other party's approval. Urgency can raise job priority and expected quote, while client reliability remains subtle so it helps fundis choose work without becoming a public shaming badge. Provenance Seal merit should be experience-weighted: a perfect first job is promising, but many completed jobs with a strong Seal rate should outrank thin history.

## Current Source Spot-Check - Fundi Cancellation, Refund And Support

- Upwork fixed-price/milestone guidance keeps escrow tied to agreed milestones and review before release: `https://support.upwork.com/hc/en-us/articles/44564821903763-Understanding-milestones-on-fixed-price-contracts`.
- Freelancer-style milestone guidance treats deposited milestone money as releaseable only through the platform flow and disputeable when the parties disagree: `https://www.freelancer.com/support/Payments/what-is-the-milestone-payment-system`.
- Taskrabbit-style local-service payment guidance keeps task payment on-platform and depends on task details/price agreed between the client and tasker: `https://support.taskrabbit.com/hc/en-us/articles/204411610-How-Do-I-Pay-My-Tasker`.

Artbook implication for this pass: cancellation should depend on job state, not on whichever party taps fastest. Before funding, close cleanly. After funding but before work starts, refund the held escrow to the customer. After work or proof starts, hold escrow and open support so evidence, room messages and the agreement decide the outcome. This keeps the customer protected without letting a customer erase work after the fundi has already started, and it keeps the fundi from forcing payout without proof and customer approval.

## Current Source Spot-Check - Mobile Touch Targets

- Android Accessibility guidance recommends touch targets at least 48dp by 48dp, with padding allowed around smaller visuals so the actual target is large enough: `https://support.google.com/accessibility/android/answer/7101858`.
- Android Developers accessibility guidance recommends each interactive UI element have a focusable/touch target of at least 48dp by 48dp: `https://developer.android.com/guide/topics/ui/accessibility/apps`.
- WCAG 2.2 Target Size (Minimum) sets a 24 CSS px Level AA floor for pointer targets, with exceptions: `https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/#target-size-minimum`.

Artbook implication for this pass: because Artbook is being installed as an Android APK, the denser chip rows and booking tabs should meet the stricter 48dp mobile target, not merely the lower WCAG AA minimum. Horizontal scrolling is acceptable for dense filters, but each chip/tab itself needs enough hit area for a thumb on the Motorola test phone.

## Current Source Spot-Check - Semantic Media Controls

- Square refund guidance limits refund action to the account owner or team members with transaction/refund-capable permissions: `https://squareup.com/help/us/en/article/5060-process-refunds`.
- Square Appointments staff guidance treats appointment access as permissioned staff work rather than something every customer can mutate: `https://squareup.com/help/us/en/article/5488-square-appointments-staff-permissions`.
- Stripe's Refund object still presents status as provider-owned payment state, which supports separating customer visibility from provider mutation authority: `https://docs.stripe.com/api/refunds/object`.

Artbook implication for this pass: customers should be able to see POS, booking and Fundi refund state, but they should not be able to mark a provider refund succeeded, failed or action-needed. The update controls belong to the seller, booked provider or assigned fundi, with staff permission checks where a POS staff model exists.

## Current Source Spot-Check - Evidence-Backed Reviews And Seals

- eBay feedback is tied to purchase history and shows verified-purchase context when the feedback relates to a completed eBay purchase: `https://www.ebay.com/help/default/default/default?id=4007`.

Artbook implication for this pass: Provenance Seals should be unlocked by completed evidence records, not by free-form goodwill. A Seal should point to a delivered order, completed booking, accepted collaboration, checked-in event or delivery proof, carry record metadata, and block duplicate vouches for the same evidence. Fundi-specific Seals keep their cooling-off and recipient-final-say rules; the general Seal path now follows the same anti-manipulation spirit.

## Current Source Spot-Check - Wallet And Android Payment Boundaries

- Google Play's Payments policy still separates Play Billing obligations for digital in-app content from non-Play payment paths for physical goods and real-world services: `https://support.google.com/googleplay/android-developer/answer/9858738`.
- Google Play's Financial Services policy treats financial products and money movement as a sensitive category that needs accurate disclosures, regulatory compliance and careful user-data handling: `https://support.google.com/googleplay/android-developer/answer/9876821`.
- Mature marketplace wallets use server-owned ledger records and provider webhooks rather than trusting phone-local balance mutations.

Artbook implication for this pass: the local wallet may show how value circulates inside Artbook, but it must stay clearly demo-only until a regulated backend exists. Internal sends should debit/credit account-scoped balances, requests should remain pending until the payer accepts, and top-up/withdraw rows should show provider-pending state. Play Store readiness needs clear digital-vs-real-world payment boundaries, KYC/AML limits, audit logs, privacy disclosures and reconciliation before any real money launches.
